Island's Fate The Quest for Survival

You are stranded on a deserted island after a shipwreck, and your only hope of survival is to navigate your way through the island to find a way to escape. Along the way, you will make crucial decisions that will determine your fate. Will you successfully escape the island, or will you be trapped forever?

About the game:

As the sole developer and designer of the game, I took great care in creating a fully original and engaging experience for the players. I made sure to only use non-copyrighted 3rd party models, which I then modified to fit my needs and the game's overall aesthetic. It was a challenging process, but one that I am proud of, and I am excited to see players enjoy the game I have created. This game, called Island's Fate: The Quest for Survival, was actually an assignment for my AET 339: Intro to Narrative class, where we had to develop a branching narrative using our knowledge of plot, character development, and storytelling. The assignment consisted of two parts: pre-writing and drafting/editing. During the pre-writing stage, we created a concept statement and beat flowchart, and during the drafting/editing phase, we developed a branching narrative with at least two branches. To keep the story engaging, we aimed for 3-5 minutes per branch, and during the editing phase, we reviewed and revised our work to ensure that it was polished and engaging. Our professor also provided a workshop for us to receive feedback from our peers. My goal was to create an adventure story with a feeling of tension and urgency. I believe that one of the strengths of my game was its simplicity, but I faced challenges in balancing the number of choices and consequences and creating two distinct and compelling branching paths. Despite the challenges, creating the game itself was a challenging and exciting experience, and I'm proud of what I created.
